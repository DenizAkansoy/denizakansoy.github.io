### Hello There!
I’m Deniz, a recent MSci Physics graduate from Imperial College London and now an MPhil student in Data Intensive Science at the University of Cambridge.

### Research Interests
I'm passionate about astrophysics, particularly planet formation, protoplanetary discs, and the search for habitable conditions in the universe.

### Previous Work
For my master’s thesis at Imperial, I explored radiative transfer in protoplanetary discs to investigate whether proto-companions could cast detectable shadows on the outer disc. Using RADMC-3D simulations, we modeled these effects to uncover potential observational signatures. Check out the [Research section](/research/) for a more in-depth explanation!

### Current Focus
At Cambridge, my MPhil work centers on applying statistical methods, high-performance computing, and machine learning to astrophysical questions.
